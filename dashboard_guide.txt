DASHBOARD GUIDE — Olist Brazilian E-Commerce
Power BI Visuals Specification
Last updated: April 2026
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Connect Power BI to olist_warehouse.db or directly to the CSV files.
Apply relationships as noted at the bottom of this file.


PAGE 1: EXECUTIVE OVERVIEW
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Goal: Quick snapshot — is the business growing and on-time?

KPI Cards (top row)
  - Total Orders: COUNT(fact_orders.order_id), filter to order_status='delivered'
  - Total Revenue (BRL): SUM(fact_orders.total_order_value)
  - Avg Order Value: AVG(fact_orders.total_order_value)
  - On-Time Delivery Rate: COUNT(is_late=0)/COUNT(*) × 100
  - Avg Review Score: AVG(fact_orders.review_score)

Revenue Over Time (Line Chart)
  X: dim_time.year + month_name (sorted)
  Y: SUM(total_order_value)
  Filter: order_status='delivered'
  Insight: Seasonality, Black Friday (November) spike expected

Orders by Status (Donut Chart)
  Values: COUNT(order_id), Legend: order_status
  Insight: High "canceled" share is a red flag

Top 5 States by Revenue (Horizontal Bar)
  X: SUM(total_order_value), Y: customer_state, Sort: DESC
  Insight: SP (São Paulo) should clearly dominate

Slicers: year, month_name, order_status, payment_type


PAGE 2: DELIVERY PERFORMANCE
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Goal: Where and when are late deliveries happening?

Late vs On-Time by Month (Stacked Bar)
  X: month+year, Y: COUNT(order_id), Stack: is_late (0=On Time, 1=Late)
  Insight: Spots seasonal delivery issues

Avg Delivery Delay by State (Filled Map)
  Location: customer_state, Color: AVG(delivery_delay_days), gradient red=high
  Insight: Northern Brazil tends to have longer delivery windows

Delivery Delay Distribution (Histogram)
  Column: delivery_delay_days, only delivered orders
  Insight: Heavy right tail = systematic delays

Late Rate by Product Category (Horizontal Bar)
  Join: fact_orders → order_items → dim_products
  X: SUM(is_late)/COUNT(*) per category, Top 10
  Insight: Heavy/bulky items or slow sellers

On-Time Rate vs Review Score (Scatter)
  X: on-time rate per category, Y: avg review_score
  Insight: Late = bad reviews, almost always

Slicers: customer_state, product_category_name_english, year, quarter


PAGE 3: PRODUCT & CATEGORY ANALYSIS
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Goal: What sells, what doesn't, and what customers think

Revenue by Category (Treemap)
  Group: product_category_name_english
  Values: SUM(order_items.price), Color: avg review_score
  Insight: Find high-revenue + high-satisfaction categories

Top 10 Products by Units Sold (Bar)
  X: COUNT(order_item_id), Y: product_id or category
  Insight: Best sellers

Review Score by Category (Horizontal Bar)
  X: AVG(review_score), Y: product_category_name_english
  Color: Green >4, Yellow 3-4, Red <3
  Insight: Low-rated categories need quality or expectation fixes

Avg Price vs Review Score (Scatter)
  X: AVG(price) per category, Y: AVG(review_score)
  Insight: Expensive doesn't always mean better rated

Items per Order (Bar)
  Column: fact_orders.item_count
  Insight: Most orders are single-item (low cross-sell)


PAGE 4: SELLER PERFORMANCE
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Goal: Who's contributing most, who's dragging down quality?

Revenue by Seller State (Map)
  Location: seller_state, Values: SUM(price)
  Insight: Most sellers in São Paulo

Top 10 Sellers by Revenue (Table)
  Columns: seller_id, seller_state, total_revenue, avg_review_score, total_orders
  Sort: total_revenue DESC

Seller Count by State (Horizontal Bar)
  X: COUNT(distinct seller_id), Y: seller_state

Avg Review Score by Seller (Distribution or Scatter)
  Segment: high >4, medium 3-4, low <3
  Insight: Identify consistently bad sellers


PAGE 5: PAYMENT BEHAVIOR
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Goal: How customers pay and how it affects the business

Payment Method Split (Donut)
  Legend: payment_type, Values: COUNT(order_id)
  Insight: Credit card dominates; boleto is meaningful

Avg Installments by Payment Type (Bar)
  X: payment_type, Y: AVG(payment_installments)
  Insight: High installments = cash flow risk for sellers

Payment Value Distribution (Histogram)
  Column: payment_value, cap at 99th percentile
  Insight: Typical order sizes

Revenue by Payment Type Over Time (Stacked Area)
  X: month/year, Y: SUM(payment_value), Stack: payment_type
  Insight: Trends — is boleto declining?


PAGE 6: CUSTOMER BEHAVIOR
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Goal: Who buys, how often, and are they coming back?

New vs Repeat Customers Over Time (Line)
  Logic: customer_unique_id with >1 order = repeat
  X: month/year, Y: count of new, count of repeat
  Insight: Repeat rate is very low (~3%) — worth monitoring

Orders by City (Top 10 Bar)
  X: COUNT(order_id), Y: customer_city

Review Score Distribution (Bar)
  X: review_score 1-5, Y: COUNT(reviews)
  Insight: Usually bimodal (5s and 1s) — interesting pattern

Customer Lifetime Value (Histogram)
  Column: SUM(total_order_value) per customer_unique_id
  Source: v_customer_summary view
  Insight: Long tail — most buy once, few buy often

POWER BI SETUP NOTES
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Connecting:
  - Install SQLite ODBC Driver, or export tables from pipeline as CSV
  - Power BI Desktop → Get Data → ODBC → select olist_warehouse.db

Relationships to define in Model view:
  fact_orders[customer_id]   → dim_customers[customer_id]   (Many-to-One)
  fact_orders[time_id]       → dim_time[time_id]            (Many-to-One)
  order_items[product_id]    → dim_products[product_id]     (Many-to-One)
  order_items[seller_id]     → dim_sellers[seller_id]       (Many-to-One)
  order_items[order_id]      → fact_orders[order_id]        (Many-to-One)
  reviews[order_id]          → fact_orders[order_id]        (Many-to-One)

Mark dim_time as the Date Table in Power BI.

Useful DAX measures:
  Late Delivery Rate =
    DIVIDE(
      COUNTROWS(FILTER(fact_orders, fact_orders[is_late] = 1)),
      COUNTROWS(fact_orders)
    )

  Repeat Customer Rate =
    DIVIDE(
      COUNTROWS(FILTER(v_customer_summary, v_customer_summary[order_count] > 1)),
      COUNTROWS(v_customer_summary)
    )

  Avg Delivery Delay (delivered only) =
    AVERAGEX(
      FILTER(fact_orders, NOT ISBLANK(fact_orders[delivery_delay_days])),
      fact_orders[delivery_delay_days]
    )

Color theme suggestion:
  Positive: #2E7D4F (dark green)
  Warning:  #F5A623 (amber)
  Negative: #C0392B (red)
  Neutral:  #2C3E50 (dark slate)
